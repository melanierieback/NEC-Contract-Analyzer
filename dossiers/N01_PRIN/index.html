Preamble
A.	Non-Extractive Capital (NEC) exists to prove, in operating form, that capital can build enterprises that ought to exist without claiming ownership of their future. The structures NEC establishes finance real ventures, share their genuine risks, take a bounded return, and dedicate everything above that bound, permanently, to the mission that raised the capital.
B.	These Principles descend from a long ethical tradition of reflection on the legitimacy of returns to capital, a tradition older than the corporation and more exacting than modern practice. They are stated here in the language of governance and law rather than in the language of any single tradition, so that any person or institution, of any conviction or none, can adopt them, verify compliance with them, and enforce them.
C.	Capital, in this understanding, is an instrument: a means of bringing into existence goods, services, and institutions that serve human flourishing and the common good. It is never an end. A structure that inverts this ordering, making the enterprise a means for the multiplication of capital, is extractive in the sense these Principles forbid, whatever its legal form.
D.	These Principles are designed for the day trust breaks, not the day it holds. Good people reduce the probability of failure; only structure contains its consequences. A perpetual undertaking cannot rest on the goodwill of persons not yet born, and so it must bind, first and hardest, its own founders. Everything in this document that appears to be addressed to a hypothetical future adversary is addressed, deliberately, to ourselves.
E.	Effect is given to these Principles through interlocking Dutch legal entities, each of which adopts them as the supreme norms of its own constitution, and through independent guardians that hold control without economics, so that no person able to act upon the structure stands to gain from unlocking it.
F.	The architecture that implements these Principles is a commons. It is published so that others may replicate, adapt, and improve it, because the purpose of proving that non-extractive capital works is that it be copied.
G.	The Adopting Entities therefore establish the following as the Non-Extractive Capital Principles, the supreme constitutional norms of the Non-Extractive Structure, from which every Constitutional Document derives its authority and to which every such document is subordinate.

Article 2. Definitions
In the Principles and in every Constitutional Document, unless mandatory law requires otherwise:
Adopting Entity: each entity listed in Schedule 1 and each entity that subsequently adopts the Principles under Article 4.
Asset Lock: the set of restrictions described in Principle 4.
Capped Return: with respect to any provision of capital, the maximum aggregate amount that may be returned to its provider, fixed ex ante in accordance with Principle 2.
Constitutional Documents: with respect to an Adopting Entity, its articles of association or equivalent constitution, together with every charter, regulation, agreement, or schedule expressed to form part of its constitutional arrangements, including the applicable Reserved Matters Schedule.
Core Assets: all assets held by or for an Adopting Entity subject to the Asset Lock, together with all income, proceeds, substitutes, and replacements of such assets.
Extraction: any transfer of value, in any form and by any mechanism, direct or indirect, from the Non-Extractive Structure to any person, beyond a Capped Return or reasonable arm's-length compensation for services actually rendered or costs actually incurred.
Guardian: the independent entity or entities holding constitutional protective rights (control without economics) in respect of an Adopting Entity, as identified in that entity's Constitutional Documents.
Locked Perimeter: dissolution and liquidation; transfers of Core Assets outside the ordinary course; encumbrance of Core Assets; merger, demerger, conversion of legal form, or cross-border relocation; amendment of Constitutional Documents; and every other matter designated a Reserved Matter.
Mission: with respect to an Adopting Entity, the purpose stated in its Constitutional Documents, as anchored in the Principles.
Non-Extractive Structure: the Adopting Entities taken together.
Principles: these Non-Extractive Capital Principles, being the Canonical Text as amended from time to time under Article 6.
Reserved Matters: the matters requiring Guardian consent, as listed in the applicable Reserved Matters Schedule, always including the Locked Perimeter and the matters captured by Article 6.4.
Steward: a natural person holding a position of control within the Non-Extractive Structure in trust for the Mission, under the qualification, selection, and accountability requirements of the Constitutional Documents.
Steward Ownership: the ownership discipline described in Principle 5.

Article 3. Canons of interpretation
3.1	The Four Questions. The Principles, and every Constitutional Document, are interpreted in the light of four questions: Who bears the loss? Who receives the surplus? Who controls time?
