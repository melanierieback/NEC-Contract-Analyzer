Clause 3. Notice, information, and consent mechanics
3.1	Advance notice: a Protected Entity that intends any act on its Locked Perimeter, or any other matter requiring a Guardian's consent or approval, gives the relevant Guardian written notice with the full file (the proposal, its reasons, the counterparties, the documents, and the assessment against the Principles) at least [thirty] days before the intended resolution, or such longer period as the matter's complexity reasonably requires.
3.2	No deemed consent: no lapse of time, incomplete response, or silence constitutes consent of a Guardian, and no Protected Entity shall rely on any purported deemed consent. 3.3	Standing information: each Protected Entity provides each Guardian: quarterly reporting on its state and its perimeter; immediate notice of every breach or attempted breach of the Principles, every approach by any person seeking a prohibited act, and every event that could impair a protective right; and every further information a Guardian requests for its function, promptly and completely.
3.4	Pending consent, the matter does not proceed; a Protected Entity that proceeds regardless is in material breach of this Charter, and every organ of every Party treats the act as ineffective to the fullest extent the law allows.
