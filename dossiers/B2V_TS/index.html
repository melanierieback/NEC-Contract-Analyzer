Legend and theological key

Five risk tiers

  • licit
  • cautionary
  • potentially extractive
  • severe
  • unclear

Six scholastic / extrinsic titles

  • Poena conventionalis - Delay may be penalized only as wrongful delay, not as the price of time.
  • Damnum emergens - The lender may be restored for actual loss, not enriched because money was lent.
  • Lucrum cessans - Foregone gain must be concrete and licit; money does not deserve growth merely because it was absent.
  • Periculum sortis - Capital may share surplus where it truly shares downside.
  • Stipendium laboris - Labor may be paid; capital should not hide yield inside labor language.
  • Carentia pecuniae - Mere lack of money is not enough; absence must not collapse into universal time-value entitlement.

Seven catechism forms

  • loan / mutuum / mutuum - Loan for consumption in which ownership of a fungible thing passes and an equivalent is owed.
  • sale / emptio venditio / emptio venditio - Exchange of a thing for a price.
  • lease / locatio conductio / locatio conductio - Hire, lease, or service relationship involving use or labor for compensation.
  • partnership / societas / societas - Association for a common enterprise with shared gain and loss.
  • gift / donatio / donatio - Transfer made gratuitously rather than for consideration.
  • pool / fundus communis (modern/constructional gesture, not a verified received scholastic term) - Shared fund or reserve for common use, risk pooling, solidarity, or mission resilience.
  • endowment / patrimonium / fundus perpetuus (modern/constructional gesture, not a verified received scholastic term) - Assets held for durable support of a mission or institution over time.

Severe signal roots

  • priority without vulnerability - Gain stripped of risk resembles the usurious claim to return without genuine periculum sortis.
  • control without stewardship - Control rights become morally disordered when they detach dominion from stewardship and the common good of the enterprise.
  • information without accountability - Information asymmetry implicates good faith in exchange and the moral problem of advantage without reciprocal responsibility.
  • liquidity without reciprocity - Commutative justice requires attention to reciprocity; liquidity rights become suspect when one party receives exit protection unavailable to others.
  • upside without downside - The societas leonina pattern: one who shares gain while avoiding loss undermines the moral form of partnership.
  • exit power without mission constraint - Exit power becomes morally dangerous when the enterprise is treated only as an alienable asset rather than a community with a purpose and mission.
